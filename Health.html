The Impact of Air Quality on Health

Air pollution is one of the greatest environmental risks to health. According to WHO, 99% of the world's population breathes air that exceeds WHO guideline limits.

NASA TEMPO Mission: Monitoring Air Pollution

NASA's TEMPO mission provides high-precision, hourly measurements of major air pollutants across North America

🌫️
Ground-Level Ozone
O₃
Forms when sunlight reacts with nitrogen oxides (NO₂) and volatile organic compounds (VOCs)
Health Effects:
  • Causes inflammation and irritation of the airways
  • Reduces lung function, especially in children and the elderly
  • Worsens asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
  • Can lead to reduced physical performance and premature death
🏭
Nitrogen Dioxide
NO₂
Produced from fuel combustion in vehicles, power plants, and industrial facilities
Health Effects:
  • Long-term exposure causes severe inflammation of the lungs
  • Increases risk of respiratory infections
  • Main component of smog that reduces visibility
  • Contributes to acid rain formation
🧪
Formaldehyde
HCHO
Released from industrial processes, vehicle emissions, and chemical production
Health Effects:
  • Known carcinogen linked to certain cancers
  • Causes eye, nose, and throat irritation
  • Can trigger asthma attacks and allergic reactions
  • Neurological effects with prolonged exposure

Global Burden of Disease from Air Pollution

Respiratory Health

Air pollution can cause or worsen asthma, bronchitis, emphysema, and other respiratory diseases.

Cardiovascular Health

Poor air quality increases the risk of heart attacks, strokes, and other cardiovascular problems.

Children's Health

Children are particularly vulnerable to air pollution, which can affect lung development and increase respiratory infections.

Protecting Your Health from Air Pollution

  • ✓ Check daily air quality forecasts and limit outdoor activities when pollution levels are high.
  • ✓ Use air purifiers at home, especially in bedrooms.
  • ✓ Wear N95 or FFP2 masks when air quality is poor, especially if you have respiratory conditions.
  • ✓ Keep windows closed during high pollution days and use air conditioning with clean filters.
  • ✓ Avoid exercising near high-traffic areas where pollution levels are typically higher.
