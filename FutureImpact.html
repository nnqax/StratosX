Breathing Clean Air

When you breathe clean air every day:

  • Better lung development in children
  • Reduced risk of asthma and allergies
  • Lower chances of heart disease
  • Improved brain function and focus
  • Longer, healthier life expectancy
  • Better sleep quality
  • More energy for daily activities
üò∑

Breathing Polluted Air

When you breathe polluted air regularly:

  • Stunted lung growth in children
  • Higher asthma and COPD rates
  • Increased heart attack risk
  • Memory problems and reduced IQ
  • Shorter lifespan by 2-3 years
  • Sleep disorders and fatigue
  • Reduced physical performance
